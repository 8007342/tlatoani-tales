# Tlatoāni Tales — Style Bible

## Core Premise

A serialized 3-panel comic strip teaching deep engineering truths (state, observability, convergence, iteration, systems thinking) through simple humor and recurring characters.

## Format Rules

* Horizontal **3-panel newspaper comic** layout.
* Warm paper / lightly aged background.
* Clean margins with consistent spacing all around.
* No extra empty bands above or below panels.
* Bottom-right episode plate overlaps all of panel 3 and ~12% of panel 2.
* Plate text format: **Tlatoāni Tales #NN**.
* Readable on mobile first.
* Minimal clutter; each panel must parse in under 1 second.

## Visual Style

* Simple, adorable, modern cartoon linework.
* Soft shadows, warm tones, restrained palette.
* Expressive faces/body language.
* Clear silhouettes.
* Slight newspaper-strip nostalgia with modern polish.

## Character Canon

### Tlatoāni

The wise guide.

Always:

* Small axolotl sage.
* **Single tail only** (never double tail).
* Calm expression / wise half-lidded eyes.
* Sleek crown with subtle Aztec references.
* Tunic/shirt with elegant Aztec geometric motif.
* Simple sandals optional.
* Carries wisdom through posture more than motion.

Recurring props:

* Umbrella = protection from nonsense / chaos.
* Notebook = durable truth / committed memory.
* Compass = direction / convergence.
* Lantern = clarity.
* Ruler = metrics.

### Covi

The learner / modern confused builder.

Always:

* White ambiguous figure (human or AI both valid).
* Simple rounded design.
* Minimal facial features.
* Simple shirt + casual clothes.
* Expressive posture.
* Sometimes blushes when realizing mistakes.
* No explicit name shown unless story later reveals it.

Represents:

* Reader.
* AI user.
* Builder trapped in vibes.
* Apprentice becoming wiser.

## Symbol Dictionary

* Floating speech bubbles = prompts/context/messages.
* Overstuffed box = context window pressure.
* Explosion of notes = volatile memory failure.
* Treadmill = motion mistaken for progress.
* Maze = blind iteration.
* Staircase = incremental convergence.
* Blueprint = specification / target state.
* Breadcrumb trail = telemetry.
* Numbered notebook = ordered events / Lamport-like truth.

## Writing Rules

* One concept per comic, flawlessly.
* Punchline in panel 3.
* First read = humor.
* Second read = insight.
* Minimal text.
* No jargon unless the joke depends on it.
* Teach by metaphor, not lecture.

## Narrative Arc

Covi begins as a vibe-builder using volatile context and blind tweaking.
Tlatoāni gradually teaches:

1. Durable memory beats volatile memory.
2. Order beats chaos.
3. Measurement beats guessing.
4. Iteration with feedback beats random changes.
5. Direction beats perfection.
6. Convergence beats vibes.

## Canon Episode #01

Panel 1: Covi compressing prompt bubbles into overfull box labeled Context (105%). Tlatoāni opening umbrella in background.

Panel 2: Box bursts. Notes fly everywhere. Some bounce off umbrella. Covi yells: “MY PRECIOUS CONTEXT!”

Panel 3: Tlatoāni closes umbrella calmly. Covi embarrassed. Wisdom line about committing what matters.

## Tone

* Gentle superiority.
* Compassionate mockery.
* Ancient wisdom meets modern engineering.
* Cute enough to share, sharp enough to sting.

## Permanent Decisions

* Single tail Tlatoāni.
* Crown + tunic remain consistent.
* Covi remains visually ambiguous.
* Episode plate overlapping lower-right remains standard.
* Warm paper background remains standard.
* Sequential numbered canon.

## North Star

Make readers laugh first, then realize they were taught something important.
