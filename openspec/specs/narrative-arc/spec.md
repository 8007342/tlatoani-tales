# Narrative Arc

## Purpose

The dramatic shape of the season. Sits above `concept-curriculum` (which is the *what*) and describes the *feel* — how Covi's confidence changes, when Tlatoāni reveals more of themselves, where the emotional beats land.

## Season 1: "From Vibes to Convergence"

### Covi's arc

- **Acts 1–3 (TT #01–04)**: Confident chaos. Covi thinks they're doing great. Each strip they're gently surprised. Humor comes from the gap between their confidence and reality.
- **Acts 4–7 (TT #05–08)**: Structural awakening. Covi starts asking questions. Humor shifts from "Covi is wrong" to "Covi is trying" — we laugh *with* them now.
- **Acts 8–11 (TT #09–12)**: Fluency. Covi starts recognizing patterns before Tlatoāni names them. Tlatoāni begins to smile more, speak less.
- **Finale (TT #13)**: Covi *teaches the reader* by asking the AI a phrase. Tlatoāni is present but quiet. The reader realizes they could ask the same thing.

### Tlatoāni's arc

- **Begins**: serene, reactive. Opens the umbrella, hands over the notebook.
- **Middle**: proactive but still wordless-leaning. Rotates the treadmill. Hands the telescope.
- **End**: barely speaks. The tools Tlatoāni introduced do the teaching. This is on purpose — wisdom transferred is wisdom that doesn't need a teacher present.

### Tonal rules

- Covi **never** becomes sad or discouraged. Every panel 3 leaves Covi slightly happier, not defeated. "Compassionate mockery" means the *situation* is funny, not the character.
- Tlatoāni **never** smug-lectures. The few words used should read as observation, not correction.
- No fourth-wall breaks until TT #13.

## Continuity

- Strips are standalone jokes but **consequential** — what Covi learned in TT #03 persists in TT #04's Covi.
- Recurring props appear earlier than their "teaching strip" to prime the reader (e.g. the notebook is in TT #01's background).
- Call-backs reward re-reading. Strip #11 may reference the exact composition of strip #02.
